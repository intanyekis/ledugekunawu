These Are Kim Cattrall's Highest-Grossing Movies And TV Shows

Publish date: 2024-08-22

  • The Ghost Writer - $65.5 Million
  • Police Academy - $81.1 Million
  • Masquerade - $91.5 Million
  • Star Trek VI: The Undiscovered Country - $96.9 Million
  • Porky's - $109.4 Million
  • Sex and the City 2 - $294.6 Million
  • Sex And The City - $415.2 Million

Kim Cattrall is a British-Canadian actress with an extensive and acclaimed film, television, and theater career. She first rose to prominence in the 1980s with roles in films like Porky's and Police Academy, establishing herself as a talented comedic actress. However, Cattrall became a household name for playing the iconic role of Samantha Jones on HBO's hit series Sex and the City. Over the show's six seasons and two feature films, Cattrall's portrayal of the vivacious public relations executive Samantha cemented her status as a pop culture icon.

In a career spanning over four decades, Cattrall's films have grossed over $1 billion at the worldwide box office. Here is a look back on Kim Cattrall's 8 highest-grossing movies and TV shows, ranked by their box office performance.

Crossroads - $57 Million

In this 2002 coming-of-age road trip comedy, Cattrall played Kit, the mother of Britney Spears' character Lucy. Though Crossroads received primarily negative reviews, it was a box office success largely thanks to Spears' star power at the time. Made on a budget of $12 million, the film went on to gross $57 million worldwide. While a supporting role, Cattrall's performance as the estranged mother was praised as one of the film's highlights.

The Ghost Writer - $65.5 Million

Kim Cattrall played Amelia Bly in this 2010 political thriller directed by Roman Polanski. The film centered on a ghostwriter hired to work on the memoirs of a former British Prime Minister. With a production budget of $45 million, The Ghost Writer earned $65.5 million worldwide and received strong critical acclaim. Alongside stars like Ewan McGregor and Pierce Brosnan, Cattrall gave a commanding performance as the wife of the ex-Prime Minister.

Police Academy - $81.1 Million

The 1984 comedy Police Academy marked one of Cattrall's earliest box office hits. She played cadet Karen Thompson, one of the new recruits training to be police officers. With raunchy humor and a cast of colorful characters, Police Academy became a surprise commercial success. Made for just $4.5 million, it went on to gross $81.1 million in North America alone, according to IMDB. The film's extensive popularity kicked off Cattrall’s career in Hollywood comedies.

Masquerade - $91.5 Million

In this 1988 romantic thriller, Cattrall starred opposite Rob Lowe as Olivia Lawrence, a young heiress. The pair play a couple who switch identities in a dangerous espionage plot. With exotic locales and plenty of steamy scenes between the leads, Masquerade resonated with audiences. It grossed $91.5 million worldwide against a slim $6 million budget. Cattrall turned heads with her sultry performance, establishing her as a versatile leading lady.

Star Trek VI: The Undiscovered Country - $96.9 Million

Kim Cattrall took on the role of Vulcan Lieutenant Valeris in this 1991 Star Trek film, the sixth based on the classic sci-fi series. With a budget of $27 million, the film earned $96.9 million globally, according to The Numbers. The plot involved efforts to forge peace between the Federation and the Klingons. Cattrall's performance as Valeris, who conspires to sabotage the peace process, earned praise for adding depth to the villainous character.

Porky's - $109.4 Million

This raunchy 1982 teen sex comedy marked one of Cattrall's earliest film roles. She played Miss Honeywell, the young female teacher who became the object of obsession for the hormonal high school boys. Porky's was made for just $4 million but became a sleeper hit, grossing $109.4 million as per Box Office Mojo. While a controversial film for its sexual content, Cattrall stood out for bringing nuance and wit to her role as Miss Honeywell.

Sex and the City 2 - $294.6 Million

Reprising her role as Samantha Jones, Cattrall starred in this 2010 sequel film based on the hit HBO series, Sex and the City. The movie followed Carrie, Samantha, and their friends as they vacationed in Abu Dhabi. Despite poor reviews, the film leveraged the popularity of the TV show to become a major box-office success. With a $100 million budget, it went on to gross $294.6 million worldwide. Audiences turned out once again to see Cattrall's fearless performance as the vibrant, sexually liberated Samantha.

Sex And The City - $415.2 Million

Without a doubt, Kim Cattrall's most commercially successful project has been the original Sex and the City film, released in 2008. Based on the groundbreaking HBO series, the movie adaptation continued to follow the relationships and sex lives of Carrie, Samantha, Miranda, and Charlotte. With a production budget of $65 million, the film earned a mammoth $415.2 million globally, including a domestic haul of $152.6 Million as per Rotten Tomatoes. Reprising her Emmy-winning role as Samantha for the big screen, Cattrall was once again a standout as the bold, unapologetic publicist.

From her early comedies to one of TV's most beloved franchises, Kim Cattrall has shown incredible range across her decades-long career. She has brought depth, confidence, and humor to characters that resonate with millions of fans. As one of her generation's most successful actresses, Cattrall has delivered unforgettable performances in films and shows that have grossed over $1 billion worldwide.
