O.J. Simpsons Cause Of Death Confirmed As Hes Accused Of Hiding Millions

Publish date: 2024-09-02

Highlights

  • O.J. Simpson died of prostate cancer after keeping his battle private while being in a hospice for months.
  • Despite claims of hidden millions in offshore accounts, Simpson reportedly had a net worth of only $3 million at the time of his death.
  • His friend alleges Simpson hid over $100 million, but his actual wealth was much lower due to legal battles and financial obligations.

The cause of O.J. Simpson’s death has been confirmed, but it remains unverified whether claims that he hid millions in offshore accounts is true.

TMZ has confirmed that Simpson died of prostate cancer after a secret battle with the disease. His attorney Malcolm LaVergne confirmed the news, revealing it’s the official cause listed on his death certificate.

Simpson Battled Cancer Privately While In A Hospice

Simpson died on April 10 after being in a hospice for months, unbeknownst to the public. He revealed he was battling cancer last May, but kept many details hidden from the public, including the type. His last public appearance came in January when photographers spotted him walking with a cane.

Since his passing, some bizarre accusations have come out about O.J. While his net worth was reportedly depleted by the time of his death, largely due to years of legal battles, one of his friends claimed he’d hidden millions of dollars in offshore accounts to avoid paying the family of his ex-wife and her lover, Rob Goldman.

O.J.’s Friend Claims He Hid $100 Million

An audio recording, acquired by The U.S. Sun, exposes a conversation where Simpson's long-time associate Bruce Fromong acknowledged assisting the controversial athlete in establishing "offshore accounts." Apparently, it may amount to over $100 million.

Fromong and Simpson share a complicated history. Despite their friendship, Simpson infamously robbed Fromong at gunpoint in 2007 at the Las Vegas Palace Station, resulting in Simpson's incarceration.

This incident occurred more than a decade after Simpson's acquittal of the murders of Brown and Goldman. Fromong testified against Simpson during his 2008 trial but later advocated for Simpson's 2017 parole release, expressing forgiveness towards him.

Even after his exoneration in the murder trials, Simpson encountered a wrongful death civil lawsuit initiated by the Goldman and Brown families. This legal battle resulted in the court deciding Simpson would have to remunerate $33.5 million in damages, a sum that escalated over time due to accruing interest.

Despite Fromong’s claims, Simpson is reported to have died with significantly less in his bank account. He apparently had a net worth of $3 million at the time of his death, which was much lower than it had been in the past.

At one point, it was estimated to be $38 million, though this still isn’t as high as the hundreds of millions Fromong claims he once was in possession of.

In his final years, Simpson reportedly made an annual income of $42,000 from Social Security, augmented by a pension from the Screen Actors Guild. Plus, he had a substantial NFL pension ranging between $125,000 to $300,000. His four surviving children are expected to inherit his fortune.
