Jessica Diane Stoebick And Ryan Gibbs Death News; Victims Died In Apparent Murder Suicide In Plainfield, Indiana

On Monday, officials in Plainfield, Indiana responded to a property located along the 5800 block of Grevillea Lane, which was claimed to be a case of a murder-suicide. This tragic event took place as the authorities were responding to the scene.

A burglary was reported at roughly 3:38 a.m. Eastern Standard Time, and the Plainfield Police Department responded quickly to the situation.

The lifeless remains of two adults, a man and a woman, were discovered by police enforcement upon their arrival for the investigation.

According to information provided by WTHR-TV, particularly noteworthy was the fact that a broken basement access window was found at the house.

The Indianapolis Star was informed by Deputy Chief Joe Aldridge of the Plainfield police department, who confirmed that the occurrence was a murder-suicide situation. On the other hand, the authorities did not reveal any facts regarding the manner in which the deceased person passed away.

News organizations in the area disclosed the identity of the deceased homeowner, who was named as Jessica Diane Stoebick, who was 47 years old.

It was revealed that the male victim, Ryan Gibbs, who was 41 years old, was Stoebick’s husband, who had been estranged from her.

Fortunately, in spite of the unfortunate circumstances, three teens who were there in the residence stayed unharmed and swiftly informed the authorities, as reported by WISH-TV.

Gibbs has been charged in the past, according to information gathered from online court documents concerning the case.

Since the month of April, he has been accused of a number of offenses, including invasion of privacy, intimidation, stalking, resisting law enforcement, and sharing of an intimate image, as described by the Star.

An statement of probable cause indicated that Gibbs violated a non-contact order. Additionally, the affidavit accused him of placing graphic photographs of the woman in her vicinity and at her place of employment, along with her name and contact information, as stated in the newspaper’s account.

In addition, the documents filed with the court disclosed that Gibbs was scheduled to attend a pre-trial conference on January 17, 2024.

Deputy Chief Aldridge highlighted the enormous difficulty that this investigation presents, which has a particularly significant impact on the family that was affected with the incident during the holiday season.

He invited the community to show their support for the family by keeping them in their thoughts and prayers in light of the unfortunate scenario that has arisen.
