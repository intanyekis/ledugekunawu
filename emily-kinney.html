    Emily Kinney Facts

  • Emily discovered her love for acting in early childhood by frequently performing various songs for her parent’s entertainment because they were fond of music.
  • She entered her first talent show despite her mother’s disapproval to achieve second place by singing My favorite things from The Sound of Music and lost to a tap dancer.
  • Emily entered the same talent show the following year with her parent’s support and came first by singing the hymn Jesus Loves Me.
  • She acted in 20 stage productions during her four years of college.
  • Emily thoroughly enjoyed her initial struggle of networking as an actress / musician by working in Brooklyn, NYC coffee shops because she is addicted to coffee.
  • She accidentally got her first break as an actress in a Broadway production called Spring Awakening (2008) after one of the cast members dropped out.
  • Emily finds fulfillment in any kind of writing exercise. She blogged for Backstage website in unscripted actor blogs for two years.
  • She was cast in The Walking Dead at the age of 25 in 2011 to play the role of a 16-year-old.
  • The Walking Dead writers made Emily’s singing abilities part of her character on the TV show upon discovering her musical talent. She also collaborated with her co-star, Lauren Cohan for the single The Parting Glass included in The Walking Dead: Original Soundtrack album.
  • Emily never travels without her guitar.
  • The actress decided to take up singing as a daily ritual to help her get comfortable in new, unfamiliar cities and unwind from the stress of a packed schedule of promotions and events.
  • She was cast in Train’s music video of Bulletproof Picasso (2015).
  • She always wears bracelets on her left hand. Emily also likes to fill up her left wrist with fan-made rubber band bracelets.
  • On the back of her left shoulder, Emily has a small black tattoo of a musical note piercing a heart.
  • She claims to be an obsessive list-maker as one of the ways to deal with her unpredictable routine. Working as an independent musician is another way she maintains control of her professional pace.
