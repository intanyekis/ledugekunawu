She was reared by divorced parents in a low-income household; her mother was given custody. At the age of eight, Anitta joined the Santa Luzia’s Church choir in her neighborhood at her maternal grandparents’ invitation. This was the start of her singing career. She started taking English classes when she was 11 years old and showed both a desire in pursuing an academic career and above-average talents.

After graduating from one of the most prestigious public schools in the nation, the Dom Pedro II School, Anitta attended a technical course in public administration in high school. She then received complimentary dance lessons from her mother’s dance teacher. Her brother Renan Machado oversees her career and is one of her song producers.

The teleseries Presença de Anita, which Rede Globo turned into a book in 2001, features a character named Anita, who served as the inspiration for Anitta. She made the decision to pursue an artistic profession a year later. The largest cultural association in Brazil, Associaço Paulista de Criticos de Arte, awarded her the title of Best New Artist that same year.
