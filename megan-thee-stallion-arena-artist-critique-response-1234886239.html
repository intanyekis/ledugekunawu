Megan Thee Stallion Responds To Charlamagne's &quot;Arena Artist&quot; Critique

Publish date: 2024-07-28

Megan Thee Stallion took to social media to update her hotties on everything MTS, as well as address critics who doubted her abilities to command an arena tour. The carousel was upload to Instagram on Wednesday (June 12) and addresses multiple happenings in the “Hot Girl” rapper’s life, from her pending album, the Hot Girl Summer Tour, and her dog 4oe.

“Hotties as we get ready for my FIRST ALBUM UNDER HOTGIRL PRODUCTIONS dropping 6.28 I wanna tell yall some of my favorite things that have been happening on tour SO FAR,” the 29-year-old explained. She continued to show gratitude for her tourmate GloRilla, the enthusiastic fans in each city, and her dancers.

The sixth bullet point in the lengthy caption takes a different approach. Rather than address her listeners or peers, Megan Thee Stallion takes aim at her haters writing, “Ima pop my sh*t for a second lol but people questioned if I was an arena artist anddd I mean,” closed with the shrugging shoulder emoji.

One of the Grammy Award-winner’s most outspoken critics regarding the Hot Girl Summer Tour came in radio personality Charlamagne Tha God. Last month, the 45-year-old walked back his “not an arena artist” description of the “Cocky Af” rapper after her worldwide trek proved to be successful.

“Clearly we got our answers,” the best-selling author admitted. “She sold out 13 arena stops in over 240,000 tickets across North America and Europe. Yeah so if you’re selling out arenas, you’re an arena artist.”

The Hot Girl Summer Tour began last month in Minneapolis and has brought Megan Thee Stallion, GloRilla, and a few special guests to sold-out audiences in Chicago, Atlanta, Detroit, New York City, and more. The U.S. leg will wrap in Las Vegas on June 22, 2024, with the international leg set to begin in Glasgow, Scotland on July 4, 2024.
